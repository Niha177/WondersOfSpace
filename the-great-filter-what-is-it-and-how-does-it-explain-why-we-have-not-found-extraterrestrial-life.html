The Great Filter- What is it and how does it explain why we have not found extraterrestrial life?

wondersofspace March 17, 2024

If you ever wondered why, out of the 100 billion of planets According to NASA, we haven't found aliens life yet.  This theory may just explain why. Of course, weather or not there is life outside of earth is debatable but out of all the planets that are in our universe, it seems highly unlikely that there is no life at all (this idea is also called the Fermi Paradox).

 

So, is there a reason why we are yet to find extraterrestrial life?

 

Yes there are many theories but today, we will be discussing "The Great Filter"

The great filter is the theory where in order for life to flourish and further evolve, all civilizations must past a "test." Only after passing this "test" will life will be allowed to further develop.

So what is this "test" that these civilizations must face?

These "tests" are thought to entail potentially destructive or mass extinction causing events. These can stem anywhere from global wide scale pandemics, cosmic tragedy's such as an asteroid, or even technology that may overtake civilizations.

How does this relate to us not being able to find extraterrestrial life?

Many scientists think that this great filter is blocking us to find life in two different way.

Theory #1

We passed the great filter, but none to very few (to the point where finding them is exceedingly difficult) other civilizations have.

This scenario would be most ideal for us in planet earth as we do not have to worry about the events of the Great Filter to come and get us. Of course, this does not exempt us from all natural disasters and widescale disasters but, it does mean our species will continue to live on.

If we passed the great filter, what was it?

It really could have been a multitude of of events or maybe just one specific event. Either way, we really have no way of knowing but there are a few theories

One theory is that the  The Permian–Triassic extinction event (aka the Great Dying)  could have been the Great Filter. The Great Dying was one of the biggest extinction event that occurred around 252 million years ago. What's strange about this event is that the cause is not really apparent, while there are theories, it's hard to pinpoint what caused this. Taking into account that nearly 80% of all species were wiped out, this could have been the so called "test" life had to pass. And, life did progress significantly after that point so this could be a viable Great Filter test. 

 

Theory #2

We are facing or are yet to face the great filter.

This is a much more terrifying scenario because this means that our species as a whole may be in danger. However, it does not mean certain annihilation of our species, rather, it means we have to somehow bypass the great filter if we want to survive.

There are multiple theories on what could be our "Great Filter" but here are some of them

 

Climate Chance

 

 

 

Climate change is undeniably huge problem to a point where the planet may just turn uninhabitable. According to Climate.gov, they state that 
 
"there is a 22% chance that 2024 will rank as the warmest year on record and a 99% chance that it will rank in the top five."
 
The upward trend in change throughout the years in climate is certainly cause for concern, and, it is a big enough concern that scientists consider this as a potential great filter.

Possible loss of control over AI

There is no doubt that recently, there has been a large uprise in AI revolutions and AI is getting increasingly more powerful as well as realistic. With how well AI is progressing, there could be a point in which humanity can loose control of AI, which could potentially  be the end for humanity. 

Another Pandemic

A future pandemic capable of being a filter would have to be a much more deadly pandemic than that of COVID in order for it to be capable of wiping out most, if not all, of humanity. This is a possible Great Filter especially seeing what pandemics can do to society.
 
 

Conclusion 

The Great Filter give's a viable solution to the question of "where are all of the aliens" but remember, that this is a theory and nothing is for sure. But, it is an interesting theory to consider, especially considering the fact that we had absolutely no outside contact with other civilizations yet despite the abundance of planets out there.
